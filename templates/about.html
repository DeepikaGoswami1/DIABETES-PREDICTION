About Our Diabetes Model

The DiaPredict application utilizes a machine learning model trained on the standard Pima Indians Diabetes Database. The objective of the dataset is to diagnostically predict whether or not a patient has diabetes, based on certain diagnostic measurements included in the dataset.

🔬 How It Works

Our backend relies on a trained predictive model (typically a Random Forest or Support Vector Machine) saved as a serialized object (`model.pkl`). Upon submitting your metrics on the prediction page, the system rapidly classifies the inputs to determine the risk factor associated with the provided biometric data.

📊 Required Diagnostic Measurements

Glucose Level Plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test.
Blood Pressure Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg).
BMI (Body Mass Index) Weight in kg/(height in m)^2.
Diabetes Pedigree Function A function which scores likelihood of diabetes based on family history.
Insulin Level 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml).
Age & Others General biometrics like pregnancies and skin thickness.
